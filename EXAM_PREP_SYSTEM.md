# Polish B1 ECL Exam Preparation System

## Purpose

This repo is not for random Polish study. Its purpose is to prepare for passing the Polish B1 ECL exam.

The training must be template-based, repetitive, and exam-output focused.

## Core principle

Do not scatter practice across disconnected grammar topics. Every practice item should support one of the reusable exam outputs:

1. self-introduction / personal monologue,
2. dialogue with another person,
3. image or comparison-based speaking,
4. guided writing task,
5. reading skimming and answer extraction,
6. listening answer extraction.

## Main exam-output spine

Every session should reuse the same speaking spine:

```text
Kim jestem?
Gdzie mieszkam?
Czym się zajmuję?
Jak wygląda moje miasto / mieszkanie?
Jak wygląda moja praca?
Co robię po pracy?
Co jest dla mnie ważne?
Jakie mam plany na przyszłość?
```

The learner should gradually turn this into a stable 2–3 minute answer.

## Speaking exam strategy

The learner should master three reusable speaking formats.

### 1. Core self-introduction

Used at the beginning of speaking exam and as warm-up in every session.

Target: 2–3 minutes.

### 2. Dialogue framework

Used when the examiner or partner gives a situation.

Always follow:

```text
1. Start politely.
2. Ask for details.
3. Give your opinion or need.
4. Explain why.
5. Suggest a solution.
6. Confirm the final decision.
```

### 3. Image/comparison framework

Used when pictures are given.

Always follow:

```text
1. Describe generally what you see.
2. Compare old vs modern / simple vs luxury / active vs passive / private vs public.
3. Give personal opinion.
4. Give one example from your life.
5. Finish with a conclusion.
```

## Universal comparison contexts

Use these contexts repeatedly:

- modern travel vs old travel,
- simple life vs luxury life,
- work from home vs office work,
- online shopping vs traditional shopping,
- city life vs village life,
- healthy lifestyle vs unhealthy lifestyle,
- active free time vs passive free time,
- technology helping people vs technology causing problems,
- young generation vs older generation,
- saving money vs spending money.

## High-frequency B1 topic bank

Practice vocabulary and texts around these repeated topics:

- personal information,
- city and place of living,
- home and apartment,
- work and routine,
- family and friends,
- free time and hobbies,
- sport and health,
- travel and transport,
- shopping and services,
- food and restaurants,
- technology and internet,
- education and language learning,
- future plans,
- problems and solutions.

## Session format

Every 60–75 minute session should follow:

```text
A. 5–10 min — recall old errors.
B. 10 min — core self-introduction repetition.
C. 20 min — controlled grammar in exam sentences.
D. 20 min — exam output: speaking/dialogue/writing/reading/listening.
E. 5 min — GitHub summary.
```

## Rule for vocabulary

New words should be added only if they are useful for B1 exam outputs. Prefer repeated high-frequency words over fancy vocabulary.

## Rule for grammar

Grammar is not studied separately. Grammar must immediately become usable exam sentences.

Example:

```text
Grammar: w pracy / dużo pracy / nie mam czasu
Exam output: Pracuję w firmie finansowej. Mam dużo pracy, ale moja praca jest interesująca. Czasem nie mam zbyt dużo czasu dla siebie.
```

## Reading strategy

Reading practice must train:

- skimming for general meaning,
- scanning for names, numbers, places, dates,
- answering only from the text,
- short answers, not full translation,
- recognizing distractors.

## Listening strategy

Listening practice must train:

- first listen: catch topic and keywords,
- second listen: confirm answer,
- short answers only,
- avoid writing too much,
- watch for negation, numbers, place/time phrases.

## Writing strategy

Writing practice must train two guided texts of about 100 words.

Every writing task must include all bullet points from the prompt. Do not skip any point.

Use simple structure:

```text
Greeting / opening
Point 1
Point 2
Point 3
Point 4
Closing
```

## Current training priority

1. Stabilize core self-introduction.
2. Stabilize grammar needed inside that introduction.
3. Build reusable dialogue and comparison phrases.
4. Only then expand reading/listening speed.

## Hard rule

If a mistake repeats twice, stop adding new material and drill that pattern until it becomes stable.
